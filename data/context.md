## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority (widened after final count). Magyar announced his ministerial cabinet on Apr 20 (WashPost, AP, Politico Europe, Reuters), completing formal government formation. One early cabinet tension emerged: a corporate executive was appointed agriculture minister despite campaign promises favoring small farms (Euractiv, Apr 21), signaling pragmatic policy over populist commitments. Magyar is characterized as "a man in a hurry" pressing institutional reforms rapidly. EUobserver raises the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. The Economist debates Orbán's comeback potential; DW examines his structural institutional persistence. Orbán's personal wealth — a village mansion — identified by The Times as proximate cause of his fall.

## Geopolitics
Druzhba oil pipeline resolution confirmed trilaterally: Zelenskiy announced resumption by end-April (Reuters, Apr 20); Kremlin confirmed readiness if Ukraine opens it (Reuters, Apr 21), closing the Hungary-Ukraine energy tension. Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations — reversing Orbán's role as Israel's EU shield; Politico Europe reports EU sanctions push on Israel now unblocked (Apr 21). The €90bn Ukraine loan veto dropped; EU approval proceeding (Euractiv, Euronews, Guardian, Apr 21). Magyar visited Polish PM Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing signals Budapest-Warsaw as Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's primary remaining CEE foothold as Hungary exits that role. Balkan Insight (Apr 21) reports Magyar plans to dismantle Orbán-era regional networks. US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
EU-Hungary frozen fund release agreement confirmed (Euronews, Apr 19; Guardian, Apr 21) as first concrete economic governance milestone. €90bn Ukraine loan unblocked in parallel. Moody's assessed the Tisza government as credit positive (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors significantly increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund distribution — a constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data have emerged internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
The ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Reuters, WashPost, Guardian, Politico Europe, Euronews, DW, AP — Apr 21) — the dominant rule-of-law story of the day, confronting Magyar with inherited discriminatory statutes requiring repeal. Orbán allies framed the ruling as "Brussels occupation" (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar's government response and legislative timeline remain unreported. Magyar renewed calls for Hungary's president and top judges to resign (Reuters, Apr 20). State media broadcast suspension announced alongside press freedom guarantees (Reuters, Apr 15; The Times, Apr 18). Politico Europe (Apr 21) raises early caution: an Orbán-era EU official may face unfair scapegoating — a rule-of-law warning directed at Magyar's own administration. EUobserver warns of the paradox of dismantling authoritarianism without replicating it.
