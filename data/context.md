## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority. Cabinet announced April 20 — agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises, generating the first credibility challenge (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Fidesz in post-defeat soul-searching with internal calls for organizational change. New April 22 pressure point: Magyar government seeking to suspend the EU's €1m daily asylum fine rather than comply with EU rules — EUobserver frames this as early EU-alignment test. Sports sector recalibration expected as Orbán's football investment priorities face reassessment.

## Geopolitics
Druzhba pipeline resumed trilaterally — confirmed by Zelenskiy, Kremlin, and Magyar (Reuters, DW, Euronews, Apr 21). €90bn Ukraine loan veto dropped; EU approval proceeding. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing is Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Politico Europe (Apr 22) raises skepticism about Magyar's regional ambitions, framing his Central European repositioning as risking Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach. Foreign Policy declares Trump-Orbán strongman axis has peaked. US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
