## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority (widened after final count). Cabinet announced April 20 (WashPost, AP, Politico Europe, Reuters). First credibility test emerged immediately: a corporate executive appointed agriculture minister despite small-farm campaign promises (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar presses judicial resignations, calling on Hungary's president and top judges to resign. EUobserver raises the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Magyar characterized as "a man in a hurry." Sports sector recalibration expected as Orbán's signature football investment priorities face reassessment (WashPost, AP, Apr 21). Orbán's personal wealth — village mansion — identified as proximate cause of his fall (The Times, Apr 18).

## Geopolitics
Druzhba pipeline resolution confirmed trilaterally: Zelenskiy announced resumption by end-April; Kremlin confirmed readiness (Reuters, Apr 20–21) — Hungary-Ukraine energy tension closed. €90bn Ukraine loan veto dropped; EU approval proceeding (Euractiv, Guardian, Euronews, Apr 21). Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role; EU sanctions push on Israel now unblocked (Politico Europe, Apr 21). Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing positions Budapest-Warsaw as Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's remaining CEE foothold as Hungary exits that role (WashPost, FT, Apr 20). Balkan Insight reports Magyar plans dismantling Orbán-era cross-border CEE networks. Foreign Policy declares the Trump-Orbán strongman axis has peaked (Apr 21). US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
EU-Hungary frozen fund release agreement confirmed (Euronews, Apr 19; Guardian, Apr 21). €90bn Ukraine loan unblocked in parallel. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors significantly increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund — a constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data have emerged internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Reuters, WashPost, Guardian, BBC, Politico Europe, Euronews, DW, AP — Apr 21) — dominant rule-of-law story, confronting Magyar with inherited discriminatory statutes requiring repeal. Orbán allies framed ruling as "Brussels occupation" (Euractiv). Magyar's legislative response and repeal timeline remain entirely unreported. Magyar renewed calls for president and top judges to resign (Reuters, Apr 20). State media broadcast suspension announced alongside press freedom guarantees (Reuters, Apr 15; Times, Apr 18). Politico Europe raises early caution: an Orbán-era EU official risks unfair scapegoating — a rule-of-law warning directed at Magyar's own administration. WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened despite the transition (Apr 19).
