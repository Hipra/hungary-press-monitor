## Political situation
Magyar's Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority (April 2026 election). Constitutional limits on prime ministerial terms implemented May 21, directly barring Orbán's return and addressing international power-concentration concerns. Tax reform sequenced after the new 2026 budget. The Orbán-era president continues actively resisting Magyar's resignation demands — the primary institutional friction, with no constitutional resolution reported. Early symbolic acts (Orbán office tours, ending wartime emergency) complete; governance narrative shifting from symbolism toward structural reform and fiscal policy sequencing.

## Geopolitics
Magyar's Warsaw visit (May 19–21) confirmed V4 revival as strategic priority; Tusk hailed Hungary's "return to Europe." V4 expansion beyond original four members under active pursuit. Ukraine-Hungary expert consultations on minority rights launched; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession. Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special Russian war crimes tribunal, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, returned $82M to Ukrainian citizens, and signaled readiness to sanction Patriarch Kirill. EU Russia sanctions advancing unblocked. Orbán's Israeli settler sanctions veto reversed. Slovakia under Fico repositioned as Hungary's EU disruptor successor. Hungary reinstated a food import ban on Ukraine (accidentally lapsed), contradicting pro-Ukraine signals — first policy inconsistency reported.

## Economy
New EU funds negotiation round opened May 18; Magyar targeting a Brussels deal on the €10.4bn package. His red-lines letter to von der Leyen anticipated but content undisclosed. Kallas added geopolitical conditionality — future EU aid conditional on stance toward Russia and Iran. S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Tax reform deferred until after 2026 budget finalization. Non-EU worker visa halt generating business-sector alarm over labor shortages. Severe drought constraining bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted final NIS proposal to Hungary's MOL. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy.

## Rule of law & human rights
Ziobro (fugitive former Polish justice minister) confirmed fled Hungary to US via Italy with Trump administration assistance. Magyar disclosed May 20 a second Polish-wanted fugitive may have exited via Serbia — first rule-of-law credibility question arising from Magyar's own government conduct rather than inherited Orbán failures. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of Orbán-era media mogul; competition probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Orbán's media empire fragmenting. Justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation. Balkan Insight raises unresolved question over whether state broadcaster staff qualify as journalists. Hundreds of Hungarian roads potentially contaminated with asbestos.
