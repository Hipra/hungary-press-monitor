## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority following the final vote count (Politico Europe, Reuters, Apr 18–19), formally ending Viktor Orbán's 15-year rule. Magyar is internationally characterized as "a man in a hurry," advancing his reform agenda rapidly. The Guardian identifies his decisive strategic innovation: converting loyal rural Fidesz voters rather than mobilizing traditional opposition constituencies. No cabinet appointments or detailed legislative calendar have been reported internationally. The Economist actively debates whether Orbán retains comeback potential from the opposition bench. DW examines how much Orbán influence persists structurally in Hungarian institutions despite electoral defeat.

## Geopolitics
EU officials conducted high-stakes Budapest talks; Magyar and the EU agreed to collaborate on releasing frozen EU funds (Euronews, Apr 19). Magyar has engaged Poland as primary partner for EU relations repair (Guardian, Apr 18). Orbán, now in opposition, signaled conditional approval of the €90bn EU loan to Ukraine — contingent on Druzhba oil pipeline resumption (Politico Europe, Apr 19), flagging active obstructionist potential. Ukrainian observers frame Magyar's victory as "a defeat for Putin" (Guardian). Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Rumen Radev won its parliamentary election (DW, FT, Politico, WashPost, Apr 18–19), confirmed as Moscow's primary remaining Central European partner. US-Hungary relations under Trump remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
Moody's assessed the pro-EU Tisza government as credit positive — the first formal sovereign credit signal of the transition (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors are significantly increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). The EU-Magyar agreement on releasing frozen funds marks the first concrete economic governance milestone (Reuters, Apr 17; Euronews, Apr 19). Euractiv reports richer EU states gaining advantage in the €400bn competitiveness fund distribution — a structural constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. No specific fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data have emerged internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
Magyar's government announced suspension of state media broadcasts while guaranteeing press freedom — the first concrete institutional media reform reported internationally (Reuters, Apr 15; The Times, Apr 18). The Financial Times identifies judicial reform as one of Magyar's central governing challenges alongside EU reintegration. The WSJ (Apr 19) warns democratic institutions were structurally weakened during Orbán's tenure, extending the reform challenge beyond political transition. The Times investigation frames Orbán's personal enrichment — a mansion in his home village — as a direct electoral cause of his downfall.
