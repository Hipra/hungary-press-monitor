## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar met von der Leyen in Brussels April 26 to negotiate frozen EU fund disbursement — first active bilateral since transition. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. State media broadcast suspension announced; restructuring timeline absent from coverage. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive remains credibility gap despite small-farm campaign promises. Orbán resigned parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition. Szijjártó leading organised Fidesz counter-narrative alleging foreign interference. DW identifies three structural hurdles facing Magyar: LGBTQ legislation repeal, entrenched institutional resistance, and EU fund conditionality.

## Geopolitics
€90bn Ukraine loan formally approved April 22–23 after Hungary lifted veto; 20th Russia sanctions package launched in parallel. Druzhba pipeline fully resumed trilaterally. EU enlargement negotiations for Ukraine and Moldova unblocked; Zelenskyy rejected "symbolic" EU membership at Cyprus summit (Apr 24). Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role for Israel. Patriots for Europe EP grouping continues unchanged despite Orbán's exit. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" frame deepening, though Politico warns his Central European ambitions risk Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Bulgaria's Radev assessed as Moscow's primary remaining CEE partner. US relations absent from all coverage despite ICC stance reversal.

## Economy
Magyar–von der Leyen Brussels meeting (Apr 26) marks first active negotiation on frozen EU fund disbursement. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive. Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory affecting 600 jobs with $65m charge (Apr 22). US investors cite Orbán-era regulatory unpredictability as unresolved concern. Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund, constraining Hungary's reintegration dividend. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review. No fiscal policy announcements or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Apr 21) — dominant rule-of-law story; Orbán allies framing ruling as "Brussels occupation." Magyar government repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely absent from international coverage. EUobserver raises central paradox: dismantling Orbán's power without replicating authoritarian power concentration. Hungary sought suspension of €1m daily asylum fine — EUobserver sceptical. ÁVH communist-era archive opening committed (Reuters, Apr 22). WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened. Foreign Policy published substantive analysis of institutional pathways for democratic rebuilding. Misinformation about Magyar persists post-election per Euronews. DW Romania: Romani community expresses cautious optimism about improved minority protections under Magyar government — first civil society voice in coverage.
