## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority (April 2026 election). Constitutional term limits enacted May 21, restricting prime ministers to two eight-year terms and barring Orbán's return — confirmed by Guardian, EUobserver, Euractiv, Reuters. Tax reform explicitly sequenced after 2026 budget finalization (Magyar, Reuters, May 21). The Orbán-era president continues actively resisting Magyar's resignation demands — primary institutional friction, constitutional basis still unreported internationally. Early symbolic acts complete (Orbán office tours, fences removed, wartime emergency ended); governance narrative now shifting from symbolism toward structural reform, fiscal sequencing, and credibility tests. The Economist and The Times flag supermajority risks and the "savior crashes" pattern as ongoing scrutiny vectors.

## Geopolitics
Magyar's Poland visit (May 19–21) complete; Tusk hailed Hungary's "return to Europe." V4 revival and potential expansion beyond original four members actively pursued (Politico, Euronews, Balkan Insight). Ukraine-Hungary expert consultations on minority rights launched May 18–20; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession. Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special Russian war crimes tribunal, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, returned $82M to Ukrainian citizens, signaled readiness to sanction Patriarch Kirill, and reversed Orbán's Israeli settler sanctions veto. EU Russia sanctions advancing unblocked. Slovakia under Fico repositioned as Hungary's EU disruptor successor (DW). Kaja Kallas added geopolitical conditionality: future EU aid contingent on stance toward Russia and Iran. Le Monde frames Poland visit as full diplomatic reset.

## Economy
New EU funds negotiation round opened May 18; Magyar targeting agreement on €10.4bn frozen package, pushing Brussels deal "next week" (Euronews, May 19). Magyar's red-lines letter to von der Leyen anticipated but content undisclosed. S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Tax reform deferred until after 2026 budget. Non-EU worker visa halt generating business-sector alarm over labor shortages (Reuters, May 15). Severe drought constraining bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted final NIS proposal to Hungary's MOL. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy. Ukraine food import ban reinstated after accidental lapse — first reported policy contradiction under Magyar (Euractiv, May 21), exposing agricultural lobby influence on foreign-policy-aligned decisions.

## Rule of law & human rights
Ziobro (fugitive former Polish justice minister) confirmed fled Hungary to US via Italy with Trump administration assistance (Reuters, May 18–20). Magyar disclosed May 20 a second Polish-wanted fugitive may have exited via Serbia — first rule-of-law credibility question arising from Magyar's own government conduct. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of Orbán-era media mogul; competition probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Orbán's media empire fragmenting (Reuters, May 15). Justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation. Balkan Insight raises unresolved question over whether state broadcaster staff qualify as journalists. Hundreds of Hungarian roads potentially contaminated with asbestos. Roma representation in Magyar's government highlighted by The Guardian as a pluralism signal.
