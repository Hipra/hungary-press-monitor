## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar is pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). State media broadcast suspension announced; misinformation against Magyar persists (Euronews, Apr 23). Magyar revealed deliberate kompromat-avoidance strategy (Euractiv, Apr 24). Orbán resigned his parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition — confirmed across WaPo, BBC, AP, NYT, Le Monde, Politico. Szijjártó reappeared April 23 alleging foreign interference — first organised Fidesz counter-narrative. EU capitals have shifted from post-election celebration to managed patience. Gáspár Orbán, Viktor's son, discharged from military service (Euronews, Apr 22).

## Geopolitics

Druzhba pipeline fully resumed, confirmed trilaterally. Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's role as Israel's EU shield (Politico US, Apr 21). Patriots for Europe EP grouping continues unchanged despite Orbán's exit. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" frame deepening, though Politico warns of Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics (Apr 22). Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Bulgaria's Radev consolidated Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. No reporting on US-Hungary bilateral relations. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review (Euronews, Apr 22).

Magyar government repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely absent from international coverage. Orbán allies framing ECJ ruling as "Brussels occupation." EUobserver raises central paradox: dismantling Orbán's power without replicating authoritarian concentration. ## Emerging narratives
**Orbán's formal parliamentary exit**: Convergent major-outlet coverage of Orbán resigning his seat and rebranding as opposition leader — symbolic consolidation of the transition. **Fidesz organisational crisis**: NYT Apr 25 focuses on Fidesz as an institution facing post-defeat restructuring, beyond Orbán personally. **Orbán patronage infrastructure collapse**: Euractiv (Apr 25) first reporting on think-tank disintegration — dependency on state capture rather than independent organisational strength. **Post-euphoria structural realism**: Cyprus summit coverage (Apr 24) frames EU capitals confronting harder competitiveness and institutional challenges; Politico argues Europe can no longer use Hungary as a scapegoat for its own structural failures. **Left's structural decline paradox**: Le Monde (Apr 24) surfaces the paradox that Orbán fell but the political left is receding across Central Europe. **Hungary as US democratic lesson**: Kenneth Roth in The Guardian (Apr 21) frames Orbán's defeat as instructive for American democratic resilience. **Gáspár Orbán discharged from military**: Minor but symbolically noted family-level transition detail (Euronews, Apr 22).
