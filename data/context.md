## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza-led government holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority. The presidential conflict remains the primary institutional friction: Magyar is running an intensifying "you must leave" campaign against the Orbán-era president (Euractiv, May 19), with no constitutional resolution pathway reported. The nepotism narrative (brother-in-law's withdrawn justice minister bid) remains active. Early symbolic gestures—Orbán's palaces opened, fences removed, wartime emergency ended, Roma hymn at parliament—are complete and fading from coverage. Key ministers hold legislative veto power. The Economist and Visegrad Insight warn Magyar's supermajority risks replicating the power concentration it replaced. Magyar is targeting a Brussels EU funds deal "next week" (Euronews, May 19). International framing has shifted from transition spectacle toward governance accountability and reform delivery.

## Geopolitics
Magyar's first foreign trip as PM went to Poland (May 19, Reuters/AP/WaPo/DW/Visegrad Insight), explicitly framing V4 reengagement and EU reintegration. Hungary-Ukraine bilateral consultations on Hungarian minority rights launched May 18; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession (Euronews, Politico). Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special tribunal for Russian war crimes, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, and returned $82 million to Ukrainian citizens. Politico elevates Hungary to potential EU peacemaker on Ukraine-Russia diplomacy. EU sanctions on Russia are now expected to advance unblocked (Politico, May 19). Slovakia under Fico now positioned by DW as Hungary's successor EU disruptor. The Times warns Magyar's pro-EU pivot exposes Hungarian minorities in Ukraine to Russian strikes. Hungary dropped Orbán's veto on EU sanctions against Israeli settlers (Foreign Policy, May 15).

## Economy
Magyar is pushing for an EU funds deal "next week" in Brussels (Euronews, May 19); a new round of negotiations formally opened May 18 with an EU team in Budapest. Magyar is preparing a red-lines letter to von der Leyen on the €10.4bn package. Estonia reaffirmed non-negotiable conditionality; Kaja Kallas added geopolitical conditionality—future EU aid conditional on stance toward Russia and Iran (Euractiv, May 18). S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy. The halt on non-EU worker visas (Reuters, May 15) is generating business-sector alarm. A severe drought constrains bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted a final proposal on NIS assets to Hungary's MOL.

## Rule of law & human rights
The Ziobro case acquired a direct US dimension: a Trump administration official helped secure a US visa for the fugitive former Polish justice minister (Reuters, May 18), connecting Hungarian rule-of-law coverage to US domestic politics. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of an Orbán-era media mogul; a competition watchdog probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Reuters frames Orbán's media empire as actively fragmenting. Balkan Insight (May 19) raises an accountability dilemma: whether state broadcasters deserve journalist protections or face institutional reform. Hungary's justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation; the 2026 Rainbow Map shows Hungary as improving-but-unresolved. Hundreds of Hungarian roads may be contaminated with asbestos (Euronews, May 15).
