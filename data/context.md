## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar met von der Leyen in Brussels April 26–27 to negotiate frozen EU fund disbursement — first active bilateral since transition. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. State media restructuring announced; timeline and ownership follow-up absent. Agriculture ministry corporate appointment remains a credibility gap. Orbán resigned parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition; US exile under active consideration. Fidesz facing organisational collapse — think-tank disintegration reveals depth of state-capture dependency. Szijjártó reappeared alleging foreign interference. Misinformation about Magyar persists post-election. NYT and AP characterise Fidesz as "licking its wounds" with leadership vacuum unaddressed. Hungary's business elite now pivoting away from Orbán toward Magyar's EU reintegration agenda (FT, Apr 28).

MOL pursuing ongoing acquisition talks with Serbia's NIS (Reuters, Apr 27). US-Hungary relations remain entirely absent from coverage.
