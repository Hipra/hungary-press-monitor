## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar is pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. State media broadcast suspension announced; misinformation against Magyar persists. Magyar revealed deliberate kompromat-avoidance strategy. Orbán resigned his parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition; US exile under consideration. Szijjártó leading organised Fidesz counter-narrative alleging foreign interference. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive remains Magyar's first credibility gap. Orbán associates reportedly rushing to move assets out of Hungary amid fears of legal accountability. Belgian PM De Wever warned EU capitals against overstating Orbán's exit — institutional residues may persist.

Politico warns Magyar's regional ambitions risk Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics.
