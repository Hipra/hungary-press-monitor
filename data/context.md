## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar is pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). State media broadcast suspension announced; misinformation against Magyar persists (Euronews, Apr 23). Magyar revealed deliberate kompromat-avoidance strategy as counter-intelligence discipline (Euractiv, Apr 24). Orbán resigned his parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition — confirmed across WaPo, BBC, AP, NYT, Le Monde, Politico. Szijjártó reappeared April 23 alleging foreign interference — first organised Fidesz institutional pushback. EU capitals have shifted from post-election celebration to managed patience.

No reporting on US-Hungary bilateral relations.

## Emerging narratives
**Orbán's formal parliamentary exit**: Apr 25 brings convergent major-outlet coverage of Orbán resigning his seat and rebranding as opposition leader — a symbolic consolidation of the transition not present in prior coverage. **Fidesz organisational crisis**: NYT Apr 25 focuses specifically on Fidesz as a party institution facing post-defeat restructuring, going beyond Orbán personally to the party's survival question. **Orbán institutional collapse confirmed**: Euractiv (Apr 25) first reporting on think-tank disintegration — Orbán's patronage infrastructure dependent on state capture rather than independent organisational strength. **Post-euphoria structural realism**: Cyprus summit coverage frames EU capitals as confronting harder competitiveness and institutional challenges. **Left's structural decline paradox**: Le Monde (Apr 24) surfaces the paradox that Orbán fell but the political left is receding across Central Europe. **Hungary as US democratic lesson**: Kenneth Roth in The Guardian (Apr 21) frames Orbán's defeat as instructive for American democratic resilience.
