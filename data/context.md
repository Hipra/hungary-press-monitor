## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority, with cabinet announced April 20. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility challenge (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Fidesz in post-defeat soul-searching; Orbán's institutional influence assessed as persistent (DW, Apr 17). Magyar committed to opening communist-era secret police (ÁVH) archives as transitional justice measure (Reuters, Apr 22). Gáspár Orbán discharged from military during transition (Euronews, Apr 22). Sports policy recalibration expected after Orbán's signature investment in football (WashPost/AP, Apr 21). BBC featured Hungary's post-Orbán media landscape as a standalone transition story (Apr 22).
