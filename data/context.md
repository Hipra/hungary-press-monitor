## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar met von der Leyen in Brussels April 26–27 on frozen EU fund release. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. Power-consolidation concern emerging April 28: Euractiv reports Magyar appointed a close adviser to an "enforcer role," introducing the first internal governance critique of the new administration. State media restructuring announced; ownership and editorial independence follow-up absent. Agriculture ministry appointment remains a credibility gap. Orbán offered to resign as Fidesz party chief April 28, deepening organisational collapse; US exile under active consideration. Szijjártó reappeared alleging foreign interference. Magyar proposed a June meeting with Zelenskyy on minority rights, signalling active bilateral normalization with Ukraine. Misinformation about Magyar persists post-election.

EU enlargement for Ukraine and Moldova unblocked; Zelenskyy rejected "symbolic" EU membership at Cyprus summit (Apr 24). Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role for Israel. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing active, though Politico warns of Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics. Bulgaria's Radev assessed as Moscow's primary remaining CEE partner. US-Hungary relations remain entirely absent from coverage despite ICC stance reversal.

## Economy
Magyar–von der Leyen Brussels meeting (Apr 26–27) marks first active negotiation on frozen EU fund disbursement; Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive. Hungary's business elite actively reorienting toward EU reintegration — FT (Apr 28) frames this as a deliberate collective sociological pivot. Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory affecting 600 jobs with $65m charge (Apr 22). US investors cite Orbán-era regulatory unpredictability as unresolved concern. Post-euphoria structural realism replacing early transition optimism. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review. No fiscal policy announcements or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

Magyar government repeal strategy and constitutional pathway entirely absent from all international coverage. Le Monde and EUobserver raise central paradox: dismantling Orbán's power without replicating authoritarian concentration — sharpened April 28 by Euractiv's enforcer-role story. Romani community expresses cautious optimism about minority protections (DW, Apr 27–28).
