## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority. Cabinet announced April 20 — agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises, generating the first credibility challenge (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Fidesz in post-defeat soul-searching; Orbán's institutional influence assessed as persistent (DW, Apr 17). Magyar government seeking to suspend the EU's €1m daily asylum fine rather than comply with EU rules — an early EU-alignment credibility test. Magyar committed to opening communist-era secret police (ÁVH) archives, signaling comprehensive transitional justice approach (Reuters, Apr 22).

## Geopolitics
Druzhba pipeline fully operational — confirmed trilaterally by Zelenskiy, Kremlin, and Magyar (Reuters, BBC, Guardian, FT, Apr 21–22). €90bn Ukraine loan veto dropped; EU approval confirmed April 22 across all major outlets. Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role and unblocking EU sanctions discussions (Politico US, Apr 21). Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing is Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor, though Politico Europe (Apr 22) warns this risks Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics. Balkan Insight reports Magyar plans active dismantlement of Orbán's cross-border CEE networks. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Foreign Policy declares Trump-Orbán strongman axis has peaked. US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
EU-Hungary frozen fund release agreement confirmed and actively negotiated (Euronews, Apr 19; Reuters, Apr 17). €90bn Ukraine loan unblocked April 22 in parallel. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors significantly increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund — a constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. Negative counter-signal: Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory with a $65m charge (WSJ, Apr 22), representing significant manufacturing divestment during the transition. No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Reuters, WashPost, Guardian, BBC, Politico, Euronews, DW, AP — Apr 21) — dominant rule-of-law story of the week. Magyar government repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely absent from international coverage. Magyar renewed calls for president and top judges to resign (Reuters, Apr 20). ÁVH archive opening committed to as transitional justice measure (Reuters, Apr 22). State media broadcast suspension announced alongside press freedom guarantees (Reuters, Apr 15). Politico Europe cautions against scapegoating Orbán-era EU officials. WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened. Asylum fine suspension bid draws EUobserver skepticism. Corruption prosecutions of Orbán-era figures publicly demanded (The Times, Apr 18) but no proceedings reported.

## Emerging narratives
**ÁVH archive opening as transitional justice signal**: Magyar's commitment to opening communist-era secret police files (Reuters, Apr 22) introduces a historical accountability dimension absent from earlier transition coverage — extending reform beyond Orbán's legacy to deeper authoritarian history. **Poland as cautionary template, not just inspiration**: Balkan Insight (Apr 22) reframes the Warsaw-Budapest comparison from aspirational to analytical, examining what pitfalls Hungary should avoid from Poland's EU reintegration experience rather than simply what to replicate. **Austro-Hungarian overreach optics**: Politico Europe (Apr 22) introduces the first critical framing of Magyar's foreign policy vision itself — not Orbán's legacy — suggesting regional ambitions carry imperial consolidation echoes. **Selective EU alignment consolidating as pattern**: Asylum fine suspension plus agriculture appointment plus Druzhba energy pragmatism begins forming a "pragmatic over principled" recurring narrative about Magyar's actual EU compliance posture.
