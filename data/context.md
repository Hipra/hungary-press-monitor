## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority following the April 2026 election. Constitutional term limits enacted May 21 bar prime ministers from serving more than two eight-year terms, constitutionally preventing Orbán's return — confirmed by Guardian, EUobserver, Euractiv, Reuters. Tax reform explicitly sequenced after 2026 budget finalization (Magyar, Reuters, May 21). Hungary's Orbán-era president actively resists Magyar's resignation demands — the primary institutional friction point; constitutional basis remains unreported internationally. Early symbolic acts complete (Orbán palace tours, fences removed); governance narrative shifting from symbolism toward structural reform, fiscal sequencing, and EU reintegration credibility tests. The Economist and The Times continue flagging supermajority risks and the "savior crashes" pattern. Budapest's mayor publicly declared Hungary can now become a "normal European country" (EUobserver, May 18).

## Geopolitics
Magyar's Poland visit (May 19–21) complete; Tusk hailed Hungary's "return to Europe." V4 revival and potential expansion beyond original four members actively pursued. Ukraine-Hungary expert consultations on minority rights launched; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession. Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special Russian war crimes tribunal, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, returned $82M to Ukrainian citizens, signaled readiness to sanction Patriarch Kirill, and reversed Orbán's Israeli settler sanctions veto. With Orbán gone, EU is now moving to extend Russia sanctions renewal deadlines unblocked (Politico, May 19). Slovakia under Fico repositioned as Hungary's EU disruptor successor (DW). Kaja Kallas added geopolitical conditionality: future EU aid contingent on Russia/Iran stance. Le Monde frames Poland visit as full diplomatic reset. Politico positions Budapest as potential EU peacemaker on Ukraine-Russia diplomacy.

## Economy
New EU funds negotiation round opened May 18; Magyar targeting agreement on €10.4bn frozen package, pushing Brussels deal "next week" (Euronews, May 19). Magyar's red-lines letter to von der Leyen anticipated but content undisclosed. S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Tax reform deferred until after 2026 budget. Non-EU worker visa halt generating business-sector alarm over labor shortages (Reuters, May 15). Severe drought constraining bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted final NIS proposal to Hungary's MOL. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy. Ukraine food import ban reinstated after accidental lapse — first reported policy contradiction under Magyar (Euractiv, May 21), exposing agricultural lobby influence on foreign-policy-aligned decisions.

## Rule of law & human rights
Ziobro (fugitive former Polish justice minister) confirmed fled Hungary to US via Italy with Trump administration assistance (Reuters, May 18–20). Magyar disclosed May 20 a second Polish-wanted fugitive may have exited via Serbia — the first rule-of-law credibility question arising from Magyar's own government. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of Orbán-era media mogul; competition probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Orbán's media empire fragmenting (Reuters, May 15). Justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation. Balkan Insight raises unresolved question over whether state broadcaster staff qualify as journalists. Hundreds of Hungarian roads potentially contaminated with asbestos. Roma representation in Magyar's government highlighted by The Guardian as a pluralism signal.
