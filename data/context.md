## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority; cabinet announced April 20. Magyar is pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). State media broadcast suspension announced. Misinformation against Magyar persists despite electoral victory (Euronews, Apr 23). Magyar revealed his kompromat-avoidance strategy — framing political survival as deliberate counter-intelligence discipline (Euractiv, Apr 24). Foreign Policy (Apr 24) published dedicated analysis of institutional and strategic pathways for rebuilding Hungarian democracy. DW (Apr 24) identifies three structural hurdles: ECJ LGBTQ ruling requiring legislation, entrenched Orbán-era institutions, and EU fund conditionality. EU capitals have shifted posture from celebration to managed patience. Szijjártó (Orbán-era FM) reappeared Apr 23 alleging foreign interference — first organised Fidesz institutional pushback.

## Emerging narratives
**"Post-euphoria" structural realism**: Cyprus summit coverage (Politico, Apr 24) frames EU capitals as moving from celebrating Orbán's defeat to confronting harder competitiveness and institutional challenges — Hungary no longer a convenient scapegoat. **Orbán exile speculation**: Balkan Insight (Apr 24) first concrete reporting on US exile consideration, elevating accountability and legal exposure questions. **CEE far-right fragmentation**: EUobserver (Apr 23) and The Parliament Magazine (Apr 24) argue Europe's far-right is fracturing and liberal forces should exploit it — Hungary's transition as systemic opportunity. **Left's structural decline paradox**: Le Monde (Apr 24) surfaces the paradox that Orbán fell but the left is receding across Central Europe — Hungary's transition reflects liberal rather than left-wing realignment. **Hungary-Poland comparative lens**: Balkan Insight (Apr 23) substantively develops the Poland democratic-transition model as both template and cautionary tale for Magyar. **Austro-Hungarian overreach optics**: Politico (Apr 22) first to explicitly name the imperial resonance risk in Magyar's regional diplomacy framing.
