## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority; cabinet announced April 20. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. DW identifies three structural obstacles: ECJ LGBTQ ruling, entrenched Orbán-era institutions, EU fund conditionality. Szijjártó reappeared 11 days post-election alleging foreign interference — first organised Orbán-era institutional pushback. State media broadcast suspension announced. Misinformation against Magyar persists despite electoral victory (Euronews, Apr 23). Tusk publicly called for EU patience with Magyar's government (FT, Apr 24), while Belgian PM De Wever cautioned against overstating the finality of Orbán's exit. Balkan Insight reports Orbán is considering exile to the United States.
