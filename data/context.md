## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. State media broadcast suspension announced; no follow-up on restructuring timeline reported. Magyar revealed deliberate kompromat-avoidance strategy. Orbán resigned his parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild Hungary's "national side" from opposition; US exile under active consideration per multiple outlets. Szijjártó leading organised Fidesz counter-narrative alleging foreign interference. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive remains Magyar's first credibility gap. Belgian PM De Wever warned EU capitals against overstating Orbán's exit — institutional residues may persist.

EU enlargement negotiations for Ukraine and Moldova unblocked; Zelenskyy rejected "symbolic" EU membership at Cyprus summit (Apr 24). Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role for Israel. Patriots for Europe EP grouping continues unchanged despite Orbán's exit. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" frame deepening, though Tusk's sardonic gesture toward Fico signals CEE coalition fragility. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Bulgaria's Radev assessed as Moscow's primary remaining CEE partner.

## Economy
Magyar–von der Leyen Brussels meeting (Apr 26) marks first active bilateral negotiation on frozen EU fund disbursement. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive. Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory affecting 600 jobs with $65m charge (Apr 22). US investors cite Orbán-era regulatory unpredictability as unresolved concern. Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund, constraining Hungary's reintegration dividend. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review. No fiscal policy announcements or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

Magyar government repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely absent from international coverage. EUobserver raises central paradox: dismantling Orbán's power without replicating authoritarian power concentration. Hungary sought suspension of €1m daily asylum fine — EUobserver sceptical. ÁVH communist-era archive opening committed. WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened.
