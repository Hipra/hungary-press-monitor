Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar met von der Leyen in Brussels April 26–27 on frozen EU fund release. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. First internal governance critique emerged April 28: Euractiv reports Magyar appointed a close adviser to an "enforcer role," raising power-consolidation concerns. Government blocked asset transfers by people linked to an Orbán aide — first concrete anti-corruption enforcement action. State media restructuring announced; ownership and editorial independence follow-up absent. Agriculture ministry appointment remains a credibility gap. Orbán offered to resign as Fidesz party chief April 28, deepening organisational collapse; US exile under active consideration. Fidesz think-tank collapsing without state backing.
