## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar met von der Leyen in Brussels April 26–27 on frozen EU fund release — first active bilateral since transition. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. New concern as of April 28: Euractiv reports Magyar appointed a close adviser to an "enforcer role," raising power-consolidation questions. State media restructuring announced; ownership and editorial independence follow-up absent. Agriculture ministry appointment remains a credibility gap. Orbán resigned parliamentary seat April 25, vowing to rebuild the "national side" from opposition; US exile under active consideration. Fidesz organisational collapse deepening — think-tank disintegration reveals state-capture dependency. Szijjártó reappeared alleging foreign interference. Misinformation about Magyar persists post-election.

## Economy
Magyar–von der Leyen Brussels meeting (Apr 26–27) marks first active negotiation on frozen EU fund disbursement; Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive. Hungary's business elite actively reorienting toward EU reintegration — FT (Apr 28) frames this as a deliberate collective sociological pivot, first such framing in transition coverage. Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory affecting 600 jobs with $65m charge (Apr 22). US investors cite Orbán-era regulatory unpredictability as unresolved concern. Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review. Post-euphoria structural realism replacing early transition optimism. No fiscal policy announcements or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.
