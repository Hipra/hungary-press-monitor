## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority. Cabinet announced April 20 — agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises, generating the first credibility challenge (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Fidesz in post-defeat soul-searching with internal calls for organizational change. New pressure point: Magyar government seeking to suspend the EU's €1m daily asylum fine rather than comply with EU rules — EUobserver frames this as an early EU-alignment test. Sports sector recalibration expected as Orbán's football investment priorities face reassessment.

## Geopolitics
Druzhba pipeline fully operational — confirmed trilaterally by Zelenskiy, Kremlin, and Magyar (Reuters, BBC, Guardian, FT, Apr 21–22). €90bn Ukraine loan veto dropped; EU approval confirmed April 22. Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role and unblocking EU sanctions discussions. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing is Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor, though Politico Europe (Apr 22) warns this risks Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics. Balkan Insight reports Magyar plans active dismantlement of Orbán's cross-border CEE networks. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Foreign Policy declares Trump-Orbán strongman axis has peaked. US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
EU-Hungary frozen fund release agreement confirmed (Euronews, Apr 19; Guardian, Apr 21). €90bn Ukraine loan unblocked in parallel. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors significantly increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund — a constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. New negative signal: Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory with a $65m charge (WSJ, Apr 22), representing significant manufacturing divestment during the transition. No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Reuters, WashPost, Guardian, BBC, Politico, Euronews, DW, AP — Apr 21) — dominant rule-of-law story of the week. Magyar government's repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely unreported. Magyar renewed calls for president and top judges to resign (Reuters, Apr 20). State media broadcast suspension announced alongside press freedom guarantees (Reuters, Apr 15). Politico Europe cautions against scapegoating Orbán-era EU officials. WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened. Asylum fine suspension bid draws EUobserver skepticism as inconsistent with stated EU alignment. Corruption prosecutions of Orbán-era figures publicly demanded (The Times, Apr 18) but no proceedings reported.
