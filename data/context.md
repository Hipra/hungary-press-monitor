## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority (April 2026 election). Constitutional term limits on the prime ministership enacted May 21, constitutionally barring Orbán's return. Tax reform explicitly sequenced after the 2026 budget (Magyar, Reuters, May 21). The Orbán-era president continues actively resisting Magyar's resignation demands — primary institutional friction, constitutional basis unreported internationally. Early symbolic acts complete (Orbán office tours, wartime emergency ended, fences removed); governance narrative shifting from symbolism toward structural reform and fiscal sequencing. The Economist flags supermajority risks and potential power concentration replication.

## Geopolitics
Magyar's Poland visit (May 19–21) confirmed V4 revival as strategic priority; Tusk hailed Hungary's "return to Europe." V4 expansion beyond original four under active pursuit. Ukraine-Hungary expert consultations on minority rights launched; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession. Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special Russian war crimes tribunal, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, returned $82M to Ukrainian citizens, signaled readiness to sanction Patriarch Kirill, and reversed Orbán's Israeli settler sanctions veto. EU Russia sanctions advancing unblocked. Slovakia under Fico repositioned as Hungary's EU disruptor successor. Hungary reinstated the Ukraine food import ban (inadvertently lapsed) — first reported policy contradiction under Magyar. Kaja Kallas added geopolitical conditionality: future EU aid contingent on stance toward Russia and Iran.

## Economy
New EU funds negotiation round opened May 18; Magyar targeting a Brussels deal on the €10.4bn frozen package, pushing for agreement "next week." His red-lines letter to von der Leyen anticipated but content undisclosed. S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Tax reform deferred until after 2026 budget finalization. Non-EU worker visa halt generating business-sector alarm over labor shortages. Severe drought constraining bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted final NIS proposal to Hungary's MOL. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy.

## Rule of law & human rights
Ziobro (fugitive former Polish justice minister) confirmed fled Hungary to US via Italy with Trump administration assistance. Magyar disclosed May 20 a second Polish-wanted fugitive may have exited via Serbia — first rule-of-law credibility question arising from Magyar's own government conduct. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of Orbán-era media mogul; competition probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Orbán's media empire fragmenting. Justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation. Balkan Insight raises unresolved question over whether state broadcaster staff qualify as journalists. Hundreds of Hungarian roads potentially contaminated with asbestos.
