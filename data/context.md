## Political situation
Magyar's Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority following the April 2026 election. The presidency remains the primary institutional friction: Magyar's "you must leave" campaign is intensifying while the Orbán-era president actively resists resignation, with no constitutional resolution reported internationally. Coverage has fully shifted from symbolic gestures (palace tours, fences, wartime emergency, Roma hymn — now archived) to governance delivery and accountability scrutiny. The supermajority concentration-of-power risk is a recurring international frame. First domestic rule-of-law challenge: Magyar disclosed a former deputy minister sought by Poland may have escaped via Serbia under his government's watch.

## Geopolitics
Magyar's Warsaw visit (May 19–21) confirmed V4 revival as a strategic priority; Tusk hailed Hungary's "return to Europe." Magyar is pursuing V4 expansion beyond the original four members. Ukraine-Hungary expert consultations on Hungarian minority rights launched May 18–20; Magyar signaled openness to Ukraine's EU accession. Hungary joined 36 countries backing a special tribunal for Russian war crimes, summoned Russia's ambassador over border-area drone strikes, returned $82M to Ukrainian citizens, and signaled readiness to sanction Patriarch Kirill. EU Russia sanctions now expected to advance unblocked. Hungary reversed Orbán's veto on EU sanctions targeting Israeli settlers. Slovakia under Fico repositioned as Hungary's successor EU disruptor. Politico frames Hungary as a potential EU peacemaker.

## Economy
A new EU funds negotiation round opened May 18 with an EU team in Budapest; Magyar is targeting a deal "next week" in Brussels on the €10.4bn package. His red-lines letter to von der Leyen is anticipated but content remains undisclosed. Estonia reaffirmed non-negotiable conditionality; Kallas added geopolitical conditionality — future EU aid conditional on stance toward Russia and Iran, directly shaping negotiations. S&P Global assigned a negative credit outlook citing fiscal transition risks. Euro adoption by 2030 remains stated policy. The halt on non-EU worker visas is generating business-sector alarm over labor shortages. A severe drought constrains bioethanol expansion. Serbia submitted a final proposal on NIS assets to Hungary's MOL.

## Rule of law & human rights
The Ziobro case acquired a direct US dimension: a Trump administration official helped secure a US visa for the fugitive former Polish justice minister (Reuters, May 18), linking Hungarian rule-of-law coverage to US domestic politics. Magyar's May 20 disclosure of a second Polish-wanted fugitive potentially exiting via Serbia introduces a domestic accountability challenge to his own rule-of-law credibility. Hungary's Constitutional Court voided key Orbán-era tax decree provisions. Police froze assets of an Orbán-era media mogul; a competition watchdog probe targets Indamedia's Ringier acquisition; Orbán's media empire is actively fragmenting. Balkan Insight raises an unresolved dilemma over whether state broadcaster staff qualify as journalists deserving professional protections. Hungary's justice minister nominee committed to revising anti-LGBTQ+ legislation; 2026 Rainbow Map shows Hungary as improving-but-unresolved. Hundreds of Hungarian roads may be contaminated with asbestos.
