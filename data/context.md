## Political situation
Magyar's Tisza Party cabinet (two-thirds majority, formed April 20) is 9 days into consolidating power. Brussels EU funds negotiation with von der Leyen (Apr 26–27, continuing Apr 29) is the dominant governance story, with multiple outlets (FT, Politico, Euractiv) converging on Brussels maintaining firm conditionality. First internal critique has emerged: Euractiv (Apr 28) reports Magyar appointed a close adviser to an "enforcer role," introducing a power-consolidation counter-narrative. Anti-corruption enforcement began: government blocked asset transfers by people linked to an Orbán aide (Reuters, Apr 28). Orbán offered to resign as Fidesz party chief (Apr 28) and resigned his parliamentary seat (Apr 25). Guardian editorial examines the paradox of Central Europe's left-wing weakness despite Hungary's democratic transition.
