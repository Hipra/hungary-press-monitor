## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party has secured a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority following the final vote count, formally ending Viktor Orbán's 15-year rule. Magyar is internationally characterized as "a man in a hurry," advancing his reform agenda at pace. The Guardian identifies his decisive strategic innovation: converting loyal rural Fidesz voters rather than mobilizing traditional opposition constituencies, abandoning left-wing messaging to position himself as the patriotic alternative. Reuters confirms Magyar will suspend state media broadcasts while protecting press freedom. EU-Hungary talks on reintegration and democratic reforms are already underway in Budapest. No specific cabinet appointments or detailed legislative calendar has been reported.

## Geopolitics
Hungary's geopolitical alignment has shifted fundamentally. EU officials are conducting high-stakes Budapest talks on democratic reforms and reintegration terms; Magyar and the EU have agreed to collaborate on releasing frozen EU funds following weekend negotiations. Magyar has engaged Poland as a primary partner for rebuilding EU relations. Orbán's defeat is widely framed as a strategic loss for Moscow, ending Hungary's role as Russia's primary NATO ally in Central Europe. Ukrainian observers frame the result as "a defeat for Putin." Washington Post identifies Bulgaria as Russia's emerging fallback Central European partner. US-Hungary relations under the Trump administration remain entirely unreported.
