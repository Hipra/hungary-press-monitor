## Political situation
Cabinet formed April 20 under Péter Magyar (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority). Magyar is pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). State media broadcast suspension announced. Misinformation against Magyar persists despite electoral victory (Euronews, Apr 23). Magyar revealed deliberate kompromat-avoidance strategy as counter-intelligence discipline (Euractiv, Apr 24). Szijjártó reappeared April 23 alleging foreign interference — first organised Fidesz institutional pushback. EU capitals have shifted from post-election celebration to managed patience. Foreign Policy (Apr 24) and Süddeutsche (Apr 23) frame democratic restoration as gradual institutional process, not dramatic upheaval.

## Geopolitics
€90bn Ukraine loan formally approved April 22–23 — Hungary's veto lifted, confirmed across all major outlets; 20th Russia sanctions package launched in parallel. Druzhba pipeline fully resumed, confirmed trilaterally. EU enlargement negotiations for Ukraine and Moldova unblocked (Politico, Apr 23); Zelenskiy rejected "symbolic" EU membership at the April 24 Cyprus summit. Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role (Politico US, Apr 21). Patriots for Europe EP grouping continues unchanged despite Orbán's defeat. Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" frame deepening, though Politico warns of Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics (Apr 22). Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev consolidated Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. No reporting on US-Hungary relations or Trump administration response.

## Economy
€90bn Ukraine loan approval dominates (Apr 22–23). EU-Hungary frozen fund release agreed in principle. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive; international investors pricing in closer EU integration (FT). Counter-signal: Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory affecting 600 jobs with $65m charge (WSJ/Reuters, Apr 22) — significant manufacturing divestment during transition. US investors citing regulatory unpredictability from Orbán era as unresolved concern (Reuters, Apr 22). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund, constraining Hungary's reintegration dividend. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review (Euronews, Apr 22). No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

No criminal proceedings or prosecutorial reporting on Orbán-era corruption.

## Emerging narratives
**"Post-euphoria" structural realism**: Cyprus summit coverage (Politico, Apr 24) frames EU capitals as now confronting harder competitiveness and institutional challenges — Hungary no longer a convenient scapegoat for EU dysfunction. **Orbán exile speculation**: Balkan Insight (Apr 24) first concrete reporting on US exile consideration, elevating accountability and legal exposure questions. **CEE far-right fragmentation**: EUobserver (Apr 23) and The Parliament Magazine (Apr 24) argue Europe's far-right is fracturing — Hungary's transition as systemic opportunity for liberal forces. **Left's structural decline paradox**: Le Monde (Apr 24) surfaces the paradox that Orbán fell but the political left is receding across Central Europe — a liberal, not left-wing, realignment. **Hungary-Poland comparative lens**: Balkan Insight develops Poland's democratic-transition model as both template and cautionary tale. **Hungary as US democratic lesson**: Kenneth Roth in The Guardian (Apr 21) frames Orbán's defeat as instructive model for American democratic resilience. **Trump-Orbán axis decline**: Foreign Policy (Apr 21) frames the transatlantic illiberal alliance as having peaked with Hungary's transition as its inflection point.
