## Political situation
Péter Magyar's Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority, with cabinet announced April 20. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility challenge (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. Fidesz in post-defeat soul-searching; Orbán's institutional influence assessed as persistent (DW, Apr 17). Magyar committed to opening communist-era secret police (ÁVH) archives as transitional justice measure (Reuters, Apr 22). Gáspár Orbán discharged from military during transition (Euronews, Apr 22). Sports policy recalibration expected after Orbán's signature investment in football (WashPost/AP, Apr 21).

## Geopolitics
Druzhba pipeline fully operational — confirmed trilaterally by Zelenskiy, Kremlin, and Magyar (Reuters, BBC, Guardian, FT, Apr 21–22). €90bn Ukraine loan approved April 22 — Hungary's veto lifted, dominant geopolitical story of the week across all major outlets. New Russia sanctions package launched in parallel (Euronews, Reuters, Apr 22). Magyar declared Hungary must arrest Netanyahu under ICC obligations, reversing Orbán's EU shield role and unblocking EU sanctions discussions (Politico US, Apr 21). Magyar visited Tusk; "Warsaw on the Danube" framing is Hungary's primary EU re-entry anchor, though Politico Europe (Apr 22) warns of Austro-Hungarian imperial overreach optics. Hungary reviewing €16bn EU defence plan — signaling strategic flexibility on security commitments. Bulgaria's Russia-aligned Radev won elections, consolidating Moscow's remaining CEE foothold. Orbán-Fico alliance formally ended; Slovak-Hungarian minority rights tensions resurfacing. Foreign Policy declares Trump-Orbán strongman axis has peaked. US-Hungary relations remain entirely unreported.

## Economy
EU-Hungary frozen fund release actively negotiated and agreed in principle (Euronews, Apr 19; Reuters, Apr 17). €90bn Ukraine loan approved April 22. Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive (Reuters, Apr 14). International investors increasing Hungarian asset purchases, pricing in closer EU integration (FT, Apr 18). Euractiv notes richer EU states gaining structural advantage in €400bn competitiveness fund — a constraint on Hungary's reintegration dividend. Counter-signal: Electrolux announced closure of its Hungary factory with a $65m charge (WSJ, Apr 22), representing significant manufacturing divestment during transition. Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan review introduces fiscal uncertainty around security spending. No fiscal policy announcements, budget decisions, or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.

## Rule of law & human rights
ECJ ruled Hungary's anti-LGBTQ legislation violates EU law (Reuters, WashPost, Guardian, BBC, Politico, Euronews, DW, AP — Apr 21) — dominant rule-of-law story of the week. Magyar government repeal strategy and timeline remain entirely absent from international coverage. Luxembourg PM Bettel publicly called the law homophobic, signaling EU peer pressure on Magyar to repeal (Euronews, Apr 22). Magyar renewed calls for president and top judges to resign (Reuters, Apr 20). ÁVH archive opening committed (Reuters, Apr 22). State media broadcast suspension announced alongside press freedom guarantees (Reuters, Apr 15; The Times, Apr 18). Politico Europe cautions against scapegoating Orbán-era EU officials. WSJ opinion argues democratic institutions remain structurally weakened. Asylum fine suspension bid draws EUobserver skepticism. Corruption prosecutions publicly demanded (The Times, Apr 18) but no proceedings reported.

## Emerging narratives
**Poland-Hungary democratic comparison deepening**: Balkan Insight (Apr 22) published substantive comparative analysis of Hungary vs. Poland's EU reintegration paths — "Warsaw on the Danube" is evolving from a diplomatic slogan into an analytical framework with lessons and limits. **Selective EU alignment consolidating as pattern**: Asylum fine suspension + agriculture appointment + Druzhba energy pragmatism + defence plan review forms a "pragmatic over principled" recurring narrative about Magyar's actual EU compliance posture. **Orbán's regional network dismantlement**: Balkan Insight (Apr 21) reports Magyar signaling active plans to dismantle Orbán-era cross-border networks in CEE — first reporting on Magyar's offensive regional posture beyond Hungary's borders. **Hungary as global democratic resilience model**: Kenneth Roth (Guardian) and multiple US outlets explicitly frame Hungary's transition as instructive for US democratic resistance to authoritarianism — elevating Hungary from regional story to global democratic template.
