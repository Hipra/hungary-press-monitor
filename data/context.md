## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a confirmed two-thirds parliamentary majority; cabinet announced April 20. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. DW identifies three structural obstacles: ECJ LGBTQ ruling, entrenched Orbán-era institutions, EU fund conditionality. Süddeutsche Zeitung frames Hungary's transition as gradual institutional dismantling rather than dramatic rupture. ÁVH archive opening committed. Gáspár Orbán discharged from military. Szijjártó reappeared after post-election disappearance, alleging foreign interference — first organised Orbán-era institutional pushback (EUobserver, Apr 23). Patriots for Europe group in European Parliament continues unchanged despite Orbán's defeat, signalling limits of domestic electoral change on EU-level right-wing politics (Politico, Apr 23).
