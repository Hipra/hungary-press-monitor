## Political situation
Tisza Party holds a two-thirds parliamentary majority; cabinet announced April 20. Agriculture ministry appointment of a corporate executive contradicts small-farm campaign promises — Magyar's first credibility gap (Euractiv, Apr 21). Magyar pressing Hungary's president and top judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. EUobserver frames the central paradox: dismantling Orbán's concentrated power without replicating it. DW identifies three structural obstacles: ECJ LGBTQ ruling, entrenched Orbán-era institutions, EU fund conditionality. Szijjártó reappeared 11 days post-election alleging foreign interference — first organised Orbán-era institutional pushback. State media broadcast suspension announced. Misinformation against Magyar persists despite electoral victory (Euronews, Apr 23). Tusk publicly called for EU patience with Magyar's government (FT, Apr 24), while Belgian PM De Wever cautioned against overstating the finality of Orbán's exit — signalling measured EU scepticism about the speed of structural change (Euractiv, Apr 23).

## Emerging narratives
**EU patience framing**: Tusk's "be very patient with Magyar" (FT, Apr 24) introduces a new register — EU principals openly managing expectations about transition speed, not just celebrating it. **De Wever scepticism**: Belgian PM's warning against overstating Orbán's exit is the first prominent EU-capital pushback on the democratic restoration narrative, elevating institutional-residue concerns to head-of-government level. **Ukraine cash vs. membership divergence**: Euractiv's framing that Ukraine secured the loan but EU membership remains stalled introduces a wedge between Hungary's geopolitical pivot and the broader enlargement agenda. **Austro-Hungarian overreach optics**: Politico's critique of Magyar's regional ambitions as echoing imperial consolidation adds a cautionary counterpoint to the "Warsaw on the Danube" celebration.
