## Political situation
Magyar's cabinet (Tisza Party, two-thirds majority) formed April 20 is consolidating power nine days in. Brussels EU funds negotiation (Apr 26–27, von der Leyen) is the dominant governance story. Magyar pressing Hungary's president and senior judges to resign as part of judicial renewal. First internal governance critique: Euractiv (Apr 28) reports Magyar appointed close adviser to an "enforcer role," introducing a power-consolidation counter-narrative. Anti-corruption enforcement began: government blocked asset transfers by people linked to an Orbán aide (Reuters, Apr 28). State media restructuring announced; ownership and editorial independence details remain absent. EU Commission separately launched court action against Hungary over an Orbán-era retail tax (Apr 29) — a legacy enforcement complication independent of the political transition.

Moody's assessed Tisza government as credit positive. Hungary's business elite actively reorienting toward EU reintegration (FT, Apr 28). Hungary's €16bn EU defence plan under review. No fiscal policy announcements or GDP/inflation data reported internationally.
