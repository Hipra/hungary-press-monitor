## Key developments / Főbb fejlemények
### EN
- Magyar publicly defended the appointment of his brother-in-law as justice minister, citing competence over family ties.
- Both Euronews and Politico Europe published critical analyses of the appointment on May 1, signalling coordinated EU press attention.
- The appointment compounds earlier concerns about a close adviser placed in an 'enforcer role', raising a pattern of executive consolidation.
- No government statement addressed a timeline for recusal review or independent vetting of the minister.

- A botrány háttérbe szorította a folyamatban lévő uniós forrástárgyalásokat, ahol a ~10 milliárd eurós megállapodás a kormány fő gazdasági prioritása.
